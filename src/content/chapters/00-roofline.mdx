# The Roofline & the Four-Way Bound

**Where we are.** Every performance question in this guide reduces to one picture: is this work
limited by arithmetic, by memory bandwidth, by communication, or by launch/overhead? The roofline
fixes the vocabulary the rest of the book reuses.

<Invariant note="cost model (timeless)">
A device finishes an operation no faster than the slower of two clocks — the rate it does
arithmetic and the rate it streams operands from memory. For work doing $\Phi$ FLOPs while moving
$\beta$ bytes on a device with peak rate $F$ and bandwidth $\mathcal B$:
$$T \ge \max\!\left(\tfrac{\Phi}{F},\ \tfrac{\beta}{\mathcal B}\right).$$
The two are equal at the **arithmetic intensity** $I=\Phi/\beta=F/\mathcal B=I^\*$ — the device's
**ridge point**. Below it a workload is *memory-bound*; above it *compute-bound*. Add a comms roof
(for multi-device) and an overhead floor (Python/launch latency) and you have the **four-way bound**.
</Invariant>

The invariant above never expires. The *numbers* that instantiate it do — so they carry provenance:

<Renewable last_measured="2026-07-17" measured_on="Tier-0 RTX 2070 SUPER" source="https://github.com/brandon-behring/transformers-systems-lab">
Our reference model **R1-tiny** (6,327,552 params) trains at ~**82,000 tok/s** at batch 8 on the
RTX 2070 SUPER; its decode step has arithmetic intensity ≈ 1 (one weight read per token) and is
firmly **memory-bound** — the reason inference optimization later targets bytes moved, not FLOPs.
</Renewable>
